The current State and Status of Theory in Psychological Science

About this survey. Psychological science has repeatedly diagnosed persistent problems with theory: many theories remain verbal and underspecified, hypotheses are often not derived from theory, and empirical findings do not consistently constrain or refine theoretical claims. A full, detailed statement of this position—together with its background and rationale—is available as an OSF preprint: https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/2fjx4_v2.

Where the items come from. The questions in this survey are taken directly from two summary tables in the preprint. Table 1 lists diagnostic claims about the current state of theorizing and theory development in psychological science (e.g., how theories are specified, how often hypotheses are derived from theory, and how empirical results feed back into theory). Table 2 lists downstream consequences that may follow when theory development is limited (e.g., limited cumulative progress, difficulty interpreting results, and weak guidance for application).

What we ask you to do. For each item, you will provide two ratings: (1) the extent to which you agree the item is accurate in your subfield at present, and (2) the extent to which the item represents a serious problem (Table 1) or a serious cause (Table 2). The survey is designed to be brief and should take only a few minutes.

Anonymity. We do not ask for names, email addresses, or other direct identifiers; please do not enter identifying information anywhere in your responses.

Purpose and use of the data. This survey is intended as an annual “sentiment barometer” for the field. We will use the results to (i) summarize current levels of agreement and perceived severity across items, (ii) identify where views are consensual versus contested, and (iii) track changes over time across yearly waves. Results may be reported in aggregate (e.g., means/distributions by item and year, possibly stratified by broad subfield or career stage when sample sizes allow). We do not aim to evaluate individual respondents.

Public availability. A de-identified dataset of responses (and the survey materials/code) will be made publicly available after collection (e.g., via OSF/GitHub) to support transparency and year-to-year comparison.
